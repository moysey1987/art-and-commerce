Know (and please) your audience

No matter how much you and I think we know about your audience, nothing will ever beat actually observing and talking to them to understand their needs, wants, emotions, behaviours, expectations and interactions.

To achieve the ideal user experience, we recommend user research and testing activities to gather insights and identify opportunities to make the greatest impact on their lives, before and after proceeding with design and development.

Once these insights have been gathered, we turn our focus to planning the holistic experience of your web presence through information architecture, wireframing and more.

Create experiences they'll remember

Let's start from the beginning

Art and Commerce is (currently) a one-person design and development studio (g'day, I'm Nigel). I've been working in user experience and content strategy for over 10 years, helping companies like Officeworks, Metricon Homes, Crown Hotels, The Movember Foundation and many others reach, delight, and sell to their audiences. I'm currently the User Experience Lead at Tundra in Fitzroy, one of Melbourne's best digital studios.

After studying design and development for a year (in an attempt to stop myself from going crazy during the coronavirus pandemic) I started Art and Commerce. The exact moment I started it, if you want the whole story, was immediately after finishing Frank Chimero's 'The Shape of Design' in an evening over a bottle of red.

I'd always loved the theory and creative expression of design, as well as the fascinating contradiction of coding's strict precision and endless flexibility. Frank's words that night seemed to be the tipping point that pushed me over the edge. That, and the confidence I'd gained from SuperHi's courses and community throughout the year.

So, after conquering the fundamentals during that dumpster fire of a year, I decided that the best way forward was to open up the My First Side HustleÂ® playset and start a small business. Inspired by the transparency and helpfulness of HAWRAF and many others like them, I plan to run this business in the open, publishing the lessons I learn as I experience them.

So, here we are. This is going to be fun.

Art + Commerce specialise in small static websites

Think creative portfolios, social media campaign landing pages, event or festival microsites, band websites, or a small business website just like this one. If you need something along those lines, we're the studio for you. We won't just design and build any old website, we'll take the time to properly understand your goals so we design and build the right website â€” and you won't have to pay us with your first-born and a six-pack for it like you will with a large agency.

You can also utilise our skills in UX, content, design, development, or project management on their own, to help you bring your project to life in any way we can. So if you've already got a developer but need some design, or already have a design but need some content, we can still help.

Finally, a note on what we can't develop just yet, and that's predominantly CMS-driven websites like Wordpress sites (where you're able to manage and update site content on your own once it's handed over to you) or eCommerce websites like Shopify. We'll be expanding our practise to take these types of jobs on in late 2021, so do keep an eye out.
